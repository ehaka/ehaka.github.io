Proposition 3.4.

Let \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) be a basis adapted to the lower central series of a nilpotent Lie algebra \(\mathfrak{g}\text{.}\) Let \(w_1,\ldots,w_n\) be the degrees of the basis elements and let \(c_{ij}^k\in\RR\) be the structure coefficients in the basis. A linear map \(\delta\colon\mathfrak{g}\to\mathfrak{g}\) is a derivation that restricts to the identity on \(\g/[\g,\g]\) if and only if

\begin{equation} \delta(X_i) = w_iX_i + \sum_{w_j\gt w_i}a_{ij}X_j\tag{3.1} \end{equation}

such that, for each triple of indices \(i,j,k\) such that \(w_k\gt w_i+w_j\text{,}\) the coefficients \(a_{ij}\in\RR\) satisfy the linear equation

\begin{align} c_{ij}^k(w_k-w_i-w_j) = \amp\sum_{w_i\lt w_h\leq w_k-w_j}a_{ih}c_{hj}^k+\sum_{w_j\lt w_h\leq w_k-w_i}a_{jh}c_{ih}^k\tag{3.2}\\ -\amp\sum_{w_i+w_j\leq w_h\lt w_k}c_{ij}^ha_{hk}\text{.}\notag \end{align}
Proof.
