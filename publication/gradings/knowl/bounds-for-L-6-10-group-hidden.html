Let us now take into consideration the non-stratifiable Lie group \(G\) of dimension 6, whose Lie algebra is denoted as \(L_{6,10}\) in [14], with the non-trivial brackets

\begin{equation*} [X_1,X_2]=X_3\;,\;[X_1,X_3]=[X_5,X_6]=X_4\text{.} \end{equation*}

The space of Rumin forms in \(G\) is

\begin{align*} E_0^1\amp=\mathrm{span}_{C^\infty(G)}\{\theta_1,\theta_2,\theta_5,\theta_6\};\\ E_0^2\amp=\mathrm{span}_{C^\infty(G)}\{\theta_{4,5}-\theta_{1,3},\theta_{1,4},\theta_{1,5},\theta_{2,3},\theta_{2,4},\theta_{2,5}\};\\ E_0^3\amp=\mathrm{span}_{C^\infty(G)}\{\theta_{2,4,5}+\theta_{1,2,3},\theta_{2,3,4},\theta_{2,3,5},\theta_{1,3,6}-\theta_{4,5,6},\theta_{1,4,6},\theta_{1,5,6}\}. \end{align*}

For the Lie algebra \(L_{6,10}\text{,}\) the maximal grading is over \(\mathbb{Z}^3\) with the layers

\begin{align*} V_{(0,1,0)}\amp=\langle X_1\rangle,\amp V_{(0,0,1)}\amp=\langle X_2\rangle,\amp V_{(0,1,1)}\amp=\langle X_3\rangle\\ V_{(0,2,1)}\amp=\langle X_4\rangle,\amp V_{(1,0,0)}\amp=\langle X_5\rangle,\amp V_{(-1,2,1)}\amp=\langle X_6\rangle\text{.} \end{align*}

Therefore, the family of projections \(\pi \colon \ZZ^3 \to \RR_+\) parametrized by \(\pi(e_i)=a_i\text{,}\) \(i=1,2,3\text{,}\) gives us all the possible weights of left-invariant 1-forms:

\begin{align*} w(\theta_1)_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}\amp=\pi((0,1,0))=a_1;\\ w(\theta_2)_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}\amp=\pi((0,0,1))=a_2;\\ w(\theta_3)_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}\amp=\pi((0,1,1))=a_1+a_2;\\ w(\theta_4)_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}\amp=\pi((0,2,1))=2a_1+a_2;\\ w(\theta_5)_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}\amp=\pi((1,0,0))=a_3;\\ w(\theta_6)_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}\amp=\pi((-1,2,1))=2a_1+a_2-a_3. \end{align*}

The homogeneous dimension is \(Q_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}=6a_1+4a_2\text{.}\)

Let us first consider the bound for non-vanishing cohomology in degree 1. If we express

\begin{gather*} \max_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)\in\mathbb{R}^3_+}\bigg\{\frac{\delta N_{\min}(1)}{Q_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}}\bigg\}=\max_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)\in\mathbb{R}^3_+}\bigg\{\frac{\min\{a_1,a_2,a_3\}}{6a_1+4a_2}\bigg\}\text{.} \end{gather*}

as a linear optimization problem, a solver finds the solution \(\frac{1}{10}\text{,}\) obtained by choosing \(a_1=a_2=a_3=1\text{.}\) Therefore \(\ell^{q,p}H^1(G)\neq 0\) for \(\frac{1}{p}-\frac{1}{q}\lt \frac{1}{10}\text{.}\)

For the Rumin 2-forms in \(E_0^2\text{,}\) the weights in terms of \(\mathbf{a}=(a_1,a_2,a_3)\) are

\begin{align*} w(\theta_{5,6}-\theta_{1,3})_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}\amp=2a_1+a_2;\\ w(\theta_{1,6})_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}\amp=3a_1+a_2-a_3;\\ w(\theta_{2,3})_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}\amp=a_1+2a_2;\\ w(\theta_{2,5})_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}\amp=a_2+a_3;\\ w(\theta_{2,6})_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}\amp=2a_1+2a_2-a_3; \end{align*}

and we want to find

\begin{gather*} \max_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)\in\mathbb{R}^3_+}\bigg\{\frac{\delta N_{\min}(2)}{Q_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}}\bigg\} \text{.} \end{gather*}

Once we re-express this as a linear optimization problem and we feed it into a solver, we get the result \(\frac{1}{10}\text{,}\) obtained by taking \(a_1=a_2=2\) and \(a_3=3\text{.}\) Therefore \(\ell^{q,p}H^2(G)\neq 0\) for \(\frac{1}{p}-\frac{1}{q}\lt\frac{1}{10}\text{.}\)

Finally, the weights of Rumin 3-forms in \(E_0^3\) in terms of \(\mathbf{a}=(a_1,a_2,a_3)\) are

\begin{align*} w(\theta_{2,5,6}+\theta_{1,2,3})_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}\amp=2a_1+2a_2;\\ w(\theta_{2,3,5})_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}\amp=a_1+2a_2+a_3;\\ w(\theta_{2,3,6})_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}\amp=3a_1+3a_2-a_3;\\ w(\theta_{1,3,4}-\theta_{4,5,6})_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}\amp=4a_1+2a_2;\\ w(\theta_{1,4,5})_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}\amp=3a_1+a_2+a_3;\\ w(\theta_{1,4,6})_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}\amp=5a_1+2a_2-a_3;\text{.} \end{align*}

In order to find

\begin{gather*} \max_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)\in\mathbb{R}_+^3}\bigg\lbrace\frac{\delta N_{\min}(3)}{Q_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}}\bigg\rbrace \end{gather*}

we once again re-express it as a linear optimization problem and obtain the result \(\frac{1}{10}\) by taking \(a_1=a_2=a_3=1\text{,}\) so that \(\ell^{q,p}H^3(G)\neq 0\) for \(\frac{1}{p}-\frac{1}{q}\lt\frac{1}{10}\text{.}\)

Finally, by Hodge duality, we obtain the bounds for \(\ell^{q,p}\) cohomology in complementary degree, that is \(\ell^{q,p}H^4(G)\neq 0\text{,}\) \(\ell^{q,p}H^5(G)\neq 0\text{,}\) and \(\ell^{q,p}H^6(G)\neq 0\text{,}\) for \(\frac{1}{p}-\frac{1}{q}\lt\frac{1}{10}\text{.}\)
