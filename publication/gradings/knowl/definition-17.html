Definition 4.17.

Let us consider a positive grading \(\mathcal{V} : \mathfrak{g}=\bigoplus_{\alpha\in\mathbb{R}_+}V_\alpha\text{.}\) Then a left invariant 1-form \(\theta\) has weight \(\alpha\text{,}\) that is \(w(\theta)=\alpha\text{,}\) if \(\theta=X^\ast\in V_\alpha\text{.}\) In other words, \(\theta\) is the dual of a vector field belonging to the subspace \(V_\alpha\text{.}\) In general, given a left-invariant \(h\)-form, we will say that it has pure weight \(p\) if it can be expressed as a linear combination of left-invariant \(h\)-forms \(\theta_{i_1}\wedge\cdots\wedge\theta_{i_h}\) such that \(w(\theta_{i_1})+\cdots+w(\theta_{i_h})=p\text{.}\)
