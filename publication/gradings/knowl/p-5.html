For nilpotent Lie algebras over algebraically closed fields of characteristic zero, an in depth study of maximal gradings over torsion-free abelian groups was carried out in [18]. One of the main results in [18] is that for any fixed nilpotency step \(s\) and dimension \(r=\dim\mathfrak{g}/[\mathfrak{g},\mathfrak{g}]\) of the abelianization, there are only finitely many torsion-free maximal gradings of nilpotent Lie algebras that cannot be identified through automorphisms of the free nilpotent Lie algebra of step \(s\) with \(r\) generators. This finiteness of the number of maximal gradings is in contrast with the existence of an uncountable number of non-isomorphic nilpotent Lie algebras in dimension 7 and higher.
