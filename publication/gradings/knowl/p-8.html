Positive gradings have an important connection with metric geometry of homogeneous spaces. They appear directly in two distinct characterizations: first, any negatively curved homogeneous Riemannian manifold is a Heintze group [21], which is a semidirect product \(G\rtimes \mathbb{R}\text{,}\) where \(G\) is a nilpotent Lie group and the action of \(\mathbb{R}\) on \(G\) is by the one-parameter family of automorphisms associated with a positive grading. Second, any connected locally compact group admitting a contracting automorphism is a positively gradable Lie group [35]. In this latter result, the group structure and contracting automorphism may also be replaced by a metric structure and a dilation, see [6].
