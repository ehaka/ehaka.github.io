Knowing all the possible positive gradings that a nilpotent Lie algebra \(\mathfrak{g}\) admits has one further application in the realm of quasi-isometric classifications. Different positive gradings can be used to obtain better estimates in the computation of the \(\ell^{q,p}\) cohomology of a nilpotent Lie group, which is a well-known quasi-isometry invariant.
