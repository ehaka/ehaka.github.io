In the literature, see e.g. [28], there appears also the concept \(\mathbb{N}\)-gradable Lie algebra, which is a nilpotent Lie algebra that admits a grading over \(\mathbb{Z}\) with strictly positive weights. The above results imply that a Lie algebra is \(\mathbb{N}\)-gradable if and only if it is positively gradable. Indeed, for any positive grading, the convex hull of the weights of its universal realization does not contain the origin by Proposition 3.9 and hence Lemma 3.7 can be applied to find the homomorphism mapping the weights to \(\mathbb{N}\text{.}\)
