Remark 3.8.

The algorithm of TheoremÂ 1.5.ii is given by the above lemma. Indeed, suppose \(S\) is an abelian group and we have an \(S\)-grading \(\mathcal{V}\) for which we aim to find a homomorphism turning it into a positive grading. Let \(S_0\) be the subgroup generated by the weights. If some element of \(S_0\) has torsion, then no such homomorphism can exist. Otherwise, \(S_0 \cong \mathbb{Z}^m\) for some \(m \ge 1\) and we may check the convex hull of the weights \(U\) under this identification. If the origin is not contained in \(U\text{,}\) the homomorphism constructed in the above lemma works. If the origin is instead contained in \(U\text{,}\) then \(0\in f(U)\) for every homomorphism \(f \colon S \to \mathbb{Z}\text{,}\) so no homomorphism mapping all weights to positive integers can exist.
