Diagonal Heintze groups form a subset of purely real Heintze groups, which are defined by requiring the derivation to have real eigenvalues. Purely real Heintze groups are the ones for which quasi-isometry classification questions are interesting. By [11] the quasi-isometry classification problem of diagonal Heintze groups reduces to an algebraic problem of finding all the possible derivations defining non-isomorphic Heintze groups.
