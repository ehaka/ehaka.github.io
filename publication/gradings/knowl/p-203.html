Let us first consider the bound for non-vanishing cohomology in degree 1. If we express

\begin{gather*} \max_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)\in\mathbb{R}^3_+}\bigg\{\frac{\delta N_{\min}(1)}{Q_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)}}\bigg\}=\max_{(a_1,a_2,a_3)\in\mathbb{R}^3_+}\bigg\{\frac{\min\{a_1,a_2,a_3\}}{6a_1+4a_2}\bigg\}\text{.} \end{gather*}

as a linear optimization problem, a solver finds the solution \(\frac{1}{10}\text{,}\) obtained by choosing \(a_1=a_2=a_3=1\text{.}\) Therefore \(\ell^{q,p}H^1(G)\neq 0\) for \(\frac{1}{p}-\frac{1}{q}\lt \frac{1}{10}\text{.}\)
