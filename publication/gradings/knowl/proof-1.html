Proof

Let \(\mathfrak{g}=\bigoplus_{\alpha\in A}V_\alpha\) be an \(A\)-grading over some group \(A\text{.}\) Denote by \(\star\) the group operation on \(A\text{.}\) We will first show by induction that if \(\alpha_1,\ldots,\alpha_k\in\Omega\) are weights such that \([[V_{\alpha_1},V_{\alpha_2}],\ldots,V_{\alpha_k}]\neq 0\text{,}\) then the elements \(\alpha_1,\ldots,\alpha_k\) commute pairwise.

The case \(k=2\) follows by anticommutativity of the Lie bracket. Namely, if \([V_{\alpha_1},V_{\alpha_2}]\neq 0\) then we find that

\begin{equation*} 0\neq [V_{\alpha_1},V_{\alpha_2}] = -[V_{\alpha_2},V_{\alpha_1}] \subset V_{\alpha_1\star\alpha_2}\cap V_{\alpha_2\star\alpha_1}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since the layers of the grading are distinct, this shows that \(\alpha_1\star\alpha_2=\alpha_2\star\alpha_1\text{.}\)

For \(k\geq 3\) the claim follows by induction and the Jacobi identity. Denote \(W = [[V_{\alpha_1},V_{\alpha_2}],\ldots,V_{\alpha_{k-2}}]\text{.}\) If \([[W,V_{\alpha_{k-1}}],V_{\alpha_k}] \neq 0\text{,}\) then by the Jacobi identity we have

\begin{equation*} 0\neq [[W,V_{\alpha_{k-1}}],V_{\alpha_k}] = [[W,V_{\alpha_{k}}],V_{\alpha_{k-1}}] + [W,[V_{\alpha_{k-1}},V_{\alpha_k}]]\text{,} \end{equation*}

so at least one of the terms on the right is nonzero as well. If the first term is nonzero, then using the inductive assumption twice we find that the elements \(\alpha_1,\ldots,\alpha_{k-2},\alpha_k\) commute pairwise and that the elements \(\alpha_1\star\alpha_2,\alpha_3,\ldots,\alpha_{k-1},\alpha_k\) commute pairwise. If the second term is nonzero, then by the inductive assumption we find that the elements \(\alpha_1,\ldots,\alpha_{k-2},\alpha_{k-1}\star\alpha_k\) commute pairwise. In either case, one further application of the inductive assumption to \([W,V_{\alpha_{k-1}}]\) shows that the elements \(\alpha_1,\ldots,\alpha_{k-1}\) commute pairwise, and by straightforward algebraic manipulations we conclude that the elements \(\alpha_1,\ldots,\alpha_k\) commute pairwise.

Let \(\alpha_1,\ldots,\alpha_n\) be the complete set of weights of the grading. By the previous argument, the grading admits a realization over the finitely presented abelian group \(\ZZ^n / R\text{,}\) where the relations are defined through the grading by

\begin{equation*} R= \{e_i+e_j-e_k: \alpha_i\star\alpha_j=\alpha_k\text{ and }0 \neq [V_{\alpha_i},V_{\alpha_j}] \subset V_{\alpha_k} \}\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(e_1,\ldots,e_n\) are the standard basis of \(\mathbb{Z}^n\text{.}\)
