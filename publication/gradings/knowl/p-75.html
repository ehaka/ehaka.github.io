Let \(\mathcal{V}\colon \mathfrak{g}=\bigoplus_{\alpha\in A}V_\alpha\) be a realization of \(\mathcal{V}\) over a torsion-free abelian group \(A\) and let \(A^*\) be the space of homomorphisms \(A\to F\text{,}\) where \(F\) denotes the field of coefficients of the Lie algebra \(\mathfrak{g}\) under consideration. By reducing to the subgroup generated by the weights, we may assume \(A \cong \mathbb{Z}^m\) for some \(m \ge 1\text{.}\) For each \(\varphi\in A^*\) define the linear map

\begin{equation*} \delta_\varphi\colon\mathfrak{g}\to\mathfrak{g},\quad \delta_\varphi(X) = \varphi(\alpha)X\quad\forall X\in V_\alpha\text{.} \end{equation*}

We claim that \(\mathfrak t = \{\delta_\varphi : \varphi \in A^*\} \) is a split torus. Indeed, a direct computation shows that all the maps \(\delta_\varphi\) are derivations, and they are diagonalizable since by construction they are multiples of the identity on each layer \(V_\alpha\text{.}\)
