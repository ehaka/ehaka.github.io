Let \(w_1,\ldots,w_n\gt 0\) be the eigenvalues of the vectors \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) for the derivation \(\delta\text{.}\) Since the maps \(x_i\mapsto \operatorname{sign}(x_i)\abs{x_i}^{w_i}\) are all invertible, the map \(\phi\colon \RR^n\to\mathfrak{g}\) is surjective if and only if the map \(\tilde{\phi}\colon \RR^n\to\mathfrak{g}\) defined by

\begin{equation} \tilde{\phi}(x_1,\ldots,x_n)=\phi(\operatorname{sign}(x_1)\abs{x_1}^{w_1},\ldots,\operatorname{sign}(x_n)\abs{x_n}^{w_n})\tag{4.3} \end{equation}

is surjective.
