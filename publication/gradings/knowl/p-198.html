Let us describe the maximization procedure in more detail. Let \(\mathcal W : \g=\bigoplus_{n\in \ZZ^k}W_n\) be a maximal grading of \(\mathfrak g\text{.}\) As observed in RemarkÂ 3.13i, every positive grading of \(\g\) is obtained (up to automorphism) as a push-forward \(\pi^{\mathbf a}_*(\mathcal W)\) by a projection \(\pi^{\mathbf a} \colon \ZZ^k \to \RR_+\text{.}\) The projections \(\pi^{\mathbf a}\) are parametrized by the vector \(\mathbf{a}=(a_1,\ldots,a_k)\in\mathbb{R}^k_+\) given by \(a_i = \pi^{\mathbf a}(e_i)\text{,}\) \(i=1,\dots,k\text{,}\) where \(e_i\) are the standard basis vectors of \(\ZZ^k\text{.}\) In the sequel we shall identify any positive grading of \(\g\) with the corresponding vector \(\mathbf{a}\in\mathbb{R}^k_+\text{.}\) In particular, if \(\theta\) is a dual of \(X\in W_n\text{,}\) then the weight of \(\theta\) with respect to the grading \(\pi^{\mathbf a}_*(\mathcal W)\) is \(w(\theta)_{\mathbf{a}} = \pi^\mathbf{a}(n)\text{.}\) Then we want to find the solution to the following expression for each degree \(h\text{:}\)

\begin{gather*} \max_{\mathbf{a}\in\mathbb{R}^k_+}\bigg\{\frac{\min_{\theta\in E_0^h}w(\theta)_\mathbf{a}-\max_{\tilde{\theta}\in E_0^{h-1}}w(\tilde{\theta})_{\mathbf{a}}}{Q_{\mathbf{a}}}\bigg\}\text{,} \end{gather*}

where \(Q_{\mathbf{a}}\) is the homogeneous dimension of the corresponding positive grading.
