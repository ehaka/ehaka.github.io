An important example of a Lie algebra grading is the maximal grading (also known as a fine grading): a grading that does not admit any proper refinement into smaller subspaces \(V_\alpha\text{.}\) A classical example is the Cartan decomposition, which is the foundation of the representation theory and classification of semisimple Lie algebras over \(\mathbb{C}\text{,}\) see for example [24]. The (maximal) gradings of semisimple Lie algebras have seen significant interest following the study [32], see the survey [25] or the monograph [16] for an overview. A classification of maximal gradings of simple classical Lie algebras over algebraically closed fields of characteristic zero can be found in [17].
