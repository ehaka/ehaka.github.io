There are two other special types of gradings of particular interest for nilpotent Lie algebras: the positive gradings and the stratifications (also called Carnot gradings). A positive grading is a grading indexed over the reals such that in the direct sum decomposition (1.1) all the non-zero spaces \(V_\alpha\) have positive indices \(\alpha\gt 0\text{.}\) A stratification is a positive grading such that \(V_1\) generates \(\mathfrak{g}\) as a Lie algebra.
